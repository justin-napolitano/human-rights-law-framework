I've been diving into human rights law lately, and I wanted to distill my insights into something concrete. That's how my GitHub repo, [human-rights-law-framework](https://github.com/justin-napolitano/human-rights-law-framework), came to life. This isn’t your typical software project with lines of code. Instead, it features a single Word document that lays out a comprehensive framework for understanding human rights law.

## What is This Repo All About?

At its core, this repository houses a document titled "TheHumanRightsFramework.docx." It's designed to be a resource for legal scholars, practitioners, and anyone interested in the finer points of human rights legislation. 

### Why Does It Exist?

Human rights law can be convoluted. There’s a need for a structured framework that captures the complexities while remaining accessible. I created this document to provide a clear, organized way to explore these laws, making them easier to reference and analyze. Think of it as a roadmap through a landscape that's often overwhelming.

## Key Design Decisions

I wanted this project to be as straightforward as possible. The main goal was to present structured content that’s useful for both legal analysis and academic reference. Here’s how I approached the design:

- **Simplicity**: A single document means there’s no need for a complex setup or dependencies. You don't have to mess around with programming languages or frameworks. You just need Microsoft Word (or a compatible processor) to dive in.
  
- **Structured Content**: I spent time organizing the material to ensure it’s logically laid out. This makes it easier for users to find what they're looking for without being bombarded by a wall of text.

## Tech Stack

Let’s not kid ourselves here; the tech stack is super minimal:

- **Document Format**: .docx (Microsoft Word)

This simplicity is both a strength and a tradeoff. On one hand, you can open the document and start reading right away. On the other, it lacks the interactivity or data manipulation features of more complex formats or codebases.
